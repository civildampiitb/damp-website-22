HS301 - Philosophy

HS301 - Philosophy


1

Review 1

By Aastha Kapoor

Prof. Ranjan K Panda
The course content was of enormous and enduring interest.The course allows one to improve their critical thinking as one rationalizes or refutes philosophies through logical arguments. Gaining an understanding of philosophy gives a chance to learn how to ask the questions and use logic and reasoning to develop responses, thus giving us an outlook on how ancient civilizations made scientific deductions by critical thinking alone.
The course content was largely covered in the slides taught by the professor, however an extra reading of ‘A History of Philosophy by Frank Thilly’ would certainly help you grasp a better understanding of the concepts taught and fetch some extra marks.
The topics covered were -
1.Branches of philosophy: Metaphysics, Epistemology, Logic, and Ethics
2.Socrates and the Socratic Schools of Thought
3.Plato and Aristotle: Reality as Idea, Matter and Form
4.Rationalism (Descartes, Leibniz) and Empiricism (Hume, Bacon, Locke)
5.Indian Philosophy (Vedas, Upanisads)
6.Buddhism: Teaching of Buddha and Metaphysics of Hinayana, Nirvana
7.Sankhya-Yoga
None
Definitely take this course if you’re extremely interested in Philosophy. This course will help broaden the range of things you understand and enjoy. It will help understand that things aren't always as they seem and provide self-knowledge, foresight and a sense of direction in life. Also, an easy course with fairly good grading statistics with plenty of AAs and ABs and extremely low FRs
1.Assignments (5%x2) : Essays were supposed to be written based on one's opinion based on the reading of ‘A History of Philosophy by Frank Thilly’ in about 500 words. More than sufficient time was provided to complete these assignments.
2.Quiz (10%) : Completely objective. Many questions were straight from the slides taught by the professor and some from Thilly’s textbook.
3.Midsem (30%) : Objective and subjective. 15% weightage for objective questions which were directly from the slides and 15% weightage was provided for subjective questions which consisted of writing down two essays (1000 words each) on different philosophical ideologies.
4.Endsem (50%) : Completely objective. This portion mainly covered Indian philosophy, for which the professor’s slides were extremely useful
1.5/5 with 5 being the toughest
A history of Philosophy by Frank Thilly

HS307-Sociology

Review 1

By Bhuvan Aggarwal

Prof. Ahonaa Roy
There was no particular motivation for taking HS307 and more of elimination. Psychology is all about cramming and my friends had already taken this course in previous semesters so I had heard good reviews of the course in terms of content and the evaluation components.
The content started with discussing some sociological constructs like capitalism, marxism etc. After that major sociologist and their works were discussed like Hobbes, Rousseau, Weber etc.
None
Courses content was okayish not very interesting for me atleast but not too boring also, there were some interesting aspects of the content. The pace was slow in the start but around midsem it picked up pace. The grading was average and the course was on the chill side in terms of load during the semester since there were only two evaluations one of them being team project. This course would be interesting for students who have a liking for history and politics. Professor doesn’t want the students to cram the content and rather want them to research and gather knowledge. All the HSS electives are almost the same difficulty wise but if you want to minimize your course load during the semester then look out for this instructor. Just a tip: If you want good grades, be interactive in class, prof really likes that.
There were two components:
Midsem Exam (50%) - It consisted of 5 sort of open ended questions which were provided almost a week before the exam date and students were expected to have thorough research on thom through books, internet etc.Students just had to write the final answers during the exam time and submit. Any sort of plagiarism was heavily penalised.
Endsem Assignment (50%) - This was a book review and presentation. Students were divided into groups and then they had to prepare a book review and presentation on the assigned book. The format for both was given.
Easy
Sociological Theory- Bert N. Adams, R. A. SydieFile
Make sure to attend the classes and follow the book.

About

D-AMP stands for the Department Academic Mentorship Program. It comprises of two D-AMP Coordinators that oversee the program for the Civil Department and around thirty mentors. As the name suggests, we help students in their academic endeavours by providing them personal guidance and mentorship in order to smoothen their transition from freshmen year to sophomore year. As the D-AMP team, we always endeavour to go a step further and address the non academic and career related concerns of our mentees in order to provide a holistic mentorship experience.
