CE221 Solid Mechanics

CE221


1

Review 1

By Bhawna Kumari

Prof. Jayadipta Ghosh

This course is perhaps the first step towards civil engineering. All the second-year students are expected to take this course as per the regular curriculum. It is an 8 credit course that includes the study of the stresses, strains, deformation, and motion of solid materials under the action of loads or forces. It is very interesting and important in order to understand the fundamentals of other structural courses.

  • Engineering Mechanics Review
  • Concept of Stress and Strain – Tension, Compression, and Shear
  • Axially Loaded Members
  • Torsion
  • Shear Force and Bending Moment Diagrams
  • Stresses in Beams (Part A: Pure Bending)
  • Stresses in Beams (Part B: Shear Stresses in Beams and Thin-Walled Members)
  • Transformation of Stress and Strain
  • Deflection of Beams
  • Column
CE 102 (Engineering Mechanics) is a prerequisite for this course. Most of the concepts learned in CE 102 are applied in this course and having done that course properly and understanding it well will help you perform well in this course too.

For me, this course was the most interesting course among all the courses that I had done from 1st to 3rd semesters and I can clearly say that it was to a large extent because of the way our Prof. Jayadipta Ghosh taught us. He explains each and every topic very clearly and you will not feel bored in his class at all. If you attend all the lectures and tutorials, you can do very well in the course. The course content as a whole is also interesting, we need to solve numerical questions applying some come concepts of physics and mathematics and I personally enjoyed it. This course is very important in order to understand the courses in the upcoming semesters such as CE 222 and CE 317. So, try to perform well in this course as it is an 8 credit course and matters a lot not only CPI but also for understanding the upcoming structural courses.

There will be 2 quizzes each of 10% weightage, a mid-semester examination of 30% weightage, and an end semester examination of 50% weightage. You need to be very clear with the concepts as there are some questions that require the use of multiple concepts put together in order to solve them. It will be very helpful if you practice problems from the tutorials and the lecture notes. Also, practicing problems from recommended textbooks and past year papers will be very beneficial.
You will find the course very easy if you attend all the lectures and tutorials and are attentive during the classes. But, you may find it difficult if you lose track during the semesters as topics are interrelated to each other. Also, this course needs a lot of practice to solve the problems and to apply the concepts correctly. You need to be consistent to perform well.
  • Gere and Goodno – Mechanics of Materials, Cengage Learning Publishers
  • Beer, Johnston, DeWolf, Mazurek – Mechanics of Materials, McGraw Hill Publishers
  • Popov and Balan – Engineering Mechanics of Solids, Prentice Hall Publishers
  • Gere and Timoshenko – Mechanics of Materials, CBS Publishers

About Tutorials: There is a 3-hour tutorial where there will be TAs and the Prof itself. He does not mark attendance if you do not solve the specified no. of questions that he has told to do. If you are unable to solve the questions in 3 hours then he asks to complete them and show it again the next day and then only you can get the attendance. And this attendance policy for tutorials really helps a lot for doing well in this course as you do not lose pace during the sem and are on the same page as the professor throughout the sem. Also, you will have to spend less time on revision during the examinations.

This course is the foundational course in water resources engineering giving you idea about basic Fluid mechanics concepts like Fluid Statics, Kinematics and Dynamics. You also learn about different types of flows and Fluid Drag..

EE101 – Introduction to Electrical and Electronic Circuits

EE101


1

Review 1

By Varun Ginde

Prof. Siddhartha Dattagupta
This course aims to provide an introduction to the analysis of electrical and electronic circuits. It provides an exposure to the practical aspects of electrical engineering.
The course initially starts off with the analysis of simple resistance circuits using Kirchoff’s laws, Norton and Thevenin theorems, star-delta transforms. Uptil the midsem examination, the theory of semiconductors and diodes is covered alongwith s-domain analysis of complex RLC circuits. Post-midsem, operational amplifiers (OP-AMPs), BJT, MOSFET and analog circuits such as rectifiers, oscillators, amplifiers, etc are covered.
There are no pre-requisites as such but it is highly recommended to go through NCERT text books of XI and XII and revise chapters like Semiconductors, Current Electricity, Magnetism, Electromagnetic Waves and Communication Systems. Semiconductors is a particularly important chapter and should not be skipped.
The course starts off with familiar concepts and is quite easy initially. No slides are provided and it is very important to make notes and pay attention to the professor during the lectures. This includes understanding the various important graphs that are drawn and explained in class (such as Bodé plots). After a point of time, quite a few concepts are repeated for different topics. Throughout the course, application of Laplace transforms plays a big role and in order to master it, regular practice is recommended. The professor will regularly revise concepts that have been taught and practice questions that are very helpful for quiz preparations. Tutorials are extremely helpful since the professor covers a variety of questions in the tutorials. Students are required to complete a course project too, which they can choose from a variety of interesting course-related topics. Small but simple assignments will be assigned, which can easily be completed by referencing through materials covered in lectures. Post midsem, the theoretical part of the course becomes tougher. Regular revision of class notes is extremely helpful because most students tend to give up during this particular phase of the course
The exams are divided into 3 categories mainly:
  • Quizzes and Assignments – 5 quizzes are conducted throughout the semester. Submission of assignments might be part of 1 or 2 quizzes. The overall weightage is 40%, with each quiz (or quiz + assignment) having an 8% weightage. Quizzes are generally easy to score. There is no midsem per se. Quiz 3 generally takes place in the midsem slot for EE-101.
  • Endsem – The end semester examination has a weightage of 40%. It’s difficult to score in, which is why it’s recommended to do as well as possible in the quizzes
  • Viva – This has a 20% weightage. Part of the viva covers the course project allotted to you but mainly, the professor will test you on course related concepts covered in lectures. The viva is generally taken towards the end of the course.
It’s not that difficult to get a decent grade in this course. To be honest, just revising from class notes is enough to guarantee a good grade. While an AA might require some effort, it is easy to score at least a BB grade by paying attention in tutorials and regularly completing assignments. No FR’s are awarded.
  • Electric Circuits, Nillson and Riedell (9th Edition)
  • Microelectronic Circuits, Sedra and Smith (7th Edition)
Nillson-Riedell is recommended for practicing circuit analysis problems, especially s-domain problems. Sedra-Smith is very important after midsem, when more in-depth theoretical material is taught. Anyone who covers Sedra-Smith thoroughly is bound to score well in the endsems as well as the later quizzes. The professor will specifically tell you which part of each chapter is to be covered.
About the Professor - The professor knows everyone in class by name. He will be aware of what your performance is in the quizzes. Students who perform well in quizzes are likely to be asked slightly lenient questions in the viva. Every now and then, he will ask you to cover certain parts of the 2 textbooks recommended above as well as do some homework on certain concepts. It is of utmost importance that you do so since this will often be helpful for quizzes. The professor holds regular doubt solving sessions in various hostels (yes, he physically goes to every hostel) and if you have any course related queries, these sessions are a great opportunity to clear your doubts. Overall, the professor is quite lenient and tries to give you as good a grade as possible.

CE204 – Geodesy

CE 204 - Geodesy


1

Review 1

By Varun Reddy

Prof. Rao E P and Prof. Indu J
This course focuses on the importance of surveying and on the various techniques, simple to complex, that are used for surveying in Engineering. This course aims to build a foundation in the Remote Sensing Specialisation of Civil Engineering and thus is largely different from other solid and fluid mechanics core courses.
Course starts with simple concepts of Plane and Geodetic Surveying and then techniques that are used in surveying like Compass Traversing, Chain Surveying, Theodolite, Levelling, Plane Table surveying, Triangulation, Tachometric Surveying, GPS surveying etc are discussed. Usage of various Instruments that are used in these different techniques is also taught in the course.
This course does not have any other course as its prerequisite. Simple logical reasoning and good understanding of basic trigonometry are sufficient to complete this course with ease
Compared to other core courses this course is relatively easy in terms of understanding the content. The course content is mostly practical. The course is accompanied by a practical course where all the concepts taught in class over a week or so are made to be applied and understood in the practical sessions, thus making the understanding process a lot easier. It is better to understand the derivations done in the class instead of just remembering the results which helps in easily solving many problems. Prof. Rao E P is an excellent professor to teach this course, he focuses more on explaining the contents of the course along with problem solving simultaneously in the lecture so that the concept being taught can be understood better. After every practical session he takes the values recorded by a batch of students from the class itself and carries the discussion forward. He also accompanies and guides students in the practical session by discussing some things that he teaches in the class. He doesn’t provide any slides to refer to his teaching or for course content, but attending all the lectures and making notes is sufficient for the course (without even referring to any textbooks). Prof. Indu J is also an excellent professor for this course, she makes students work on derivations in the class that are involved in the course. She also interactively discusses problems in the class which involve application of a derivation or a formula taught before the problem. She also provides slides to students for reference. Overall her class is very interactive and interesting.
The questions in the exams are a mixture of theory and numerical questions. In the numerical part weightage is given to both final answer and intermediate steps. Weightage distribution for Grading is a typical one: Quizzes: 10% (2 quizzes in total), Mid-sem: 30% and End-sem: 50%
The course content in terms of ease of understanding is easy compared to the difficulty of other courses. But not concentrating in lectures or missing some lectures might result in some inconveniences due to the nature of the course.

You will get a chance to analyze structures on pen and paper, how a complex structure can be simplified into a 2-D problem and solved using basic force and equilibrium equation or with the work-energy method. You will also get to know about the type of structures, types of load that structure faces like dead load, wind load, live load, and moving loads and then you will apply this concept for deciding how much strength is required in trusses, bridges with the details of load applied given in the question for simplicity.

    Statically Determinate Trusses – Types and classification of trusses; Stability and determinacy; Sign convention and force representation; Analysis methods; Zero-force members;
    Statically Determinate Beams and Frames – Internal forces and sign convention; Load, shear and bending moment relationships; Axial, shear and moment diagrams for beams and frames; Qualitative deflected shape;
    Cables and Arches
    Influence Lines for Statically Determinate Structures – Constructing influence lines for beams under distributed load; Müller-Breslau principle; Influence lines for floor systems and trusses;
    Deflections – Defections by the method of virtual work; Applications to trusses, beams and frames; Castigliano’s theorem; Conjugate beam method;
    Analysis of Statically Indeterminate Structures – Static indeterminacy; Advantages and disadvantages of statically indeterminate structures; Compatibility conditions; Methods of Analysis; Maxwell’s theorem and Betti’s law; Force method of analysis for beams, frames and trusses;

    1

    Review 1

    By Devang Singh Saxena

    Prof.Prakash Nanthagopalan
    This course is of 6 credits and has been newly introduced into the curriculum because of its significant importance to construction-related studies. It is a theoretical course which explores every aspect related to building materials and concepts involved in every day constructional activities deeply. The motive of this course is to acquaint the student to the constructional practices that are being followed in today’s day and age globally.
    • Stones
    • Bricks
    • Cement and concrete
    • Tiles
    • Glass
    • Steel
    • Foundations
    • Construction equipments
    • Masonry
    • Pointing and plastering
    • Lintels and arches
    • Flooring and roofing
    • Water-proofing
    • Formwork and scaffolding
    NA
    This course is theoretically intensive, but provides all the knowledge that a site engineer requires for the construction of structures. It also opens a lot of interesting research opportunities for the student to explore in the field of material studies and constructional practices. In that sense it is probably one of the most useful courses for a civil engineer. It provides all the theoretical knowledge of construction practices and hence its relevance to an aspiring civil engineer is unprecedented. The course content also provides a strong foundation to future theoretical courses and labs. The course also requires that a student reads up the relevant IS codes when mentioned in the slide or by the professor
    All the questions in the exams are purely theoretical. The paper has around 30% objective questions and one line answer questions and 70% long-answer type questions. The objective questions are tougher as the marking scheme is +1 for the right answer and -1 for the wrong one. Every answer needs to contain (2xno. Of marks allotted to the question) number of relevant points, i.e if the question is of 2 marks then 4 relevant points are expected, and the evaluation is strictly on the basis of this. 80% of the questions are from those topics covered in class while 20% require the student to explore those topics more deeply, understanding the key philosophy behind each of the topic.
    Easy
    This is one of the easier, mentally-less challenging courses which demand a lot of time to be invested while studying for it as it involves memorizing stuff to an extent. But, rest assured that this won’t be like memorising stuff for board-exams, it will be a lot more interesting and practically applicable material that you would be going through and memorizing.
    There are no books to be followed as such, hence class noted is very important. Just pay attention in class, the professor will provide internet references to many concepts, just note them down and do go through them as they are generally quite interesting and are also not as lengthy as books!
    Make notes in the class as most of the questions asked in the exam are from what the professor teaches in class additional to the slides and that might just increase your popularity among the rest of your sleeping batch-mates!
