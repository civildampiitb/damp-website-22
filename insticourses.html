GNR638 - Machine Learning for Remote Sensing 2

GNR638 - Machine Learning for Remote Sensing 2


1

Review 1

By Bhuvan Aggarwal

Prof. Biplab Banerjee
Its a practical ML course which gives a nice overview of all branches of machine learning and deep learning architectures like convnets, RNNs, Autoencoders and so on. It is a nice course for intern/placement preperation as it will give you knowledge and some ML projects. One more benefit is that it doesn't go into the uncessary mathematics which some other introductory courses do. Moreover it is in the basket for DS minor.
Image recognition using bag of words and deep learning methods. Object detection, Image segmentation, some basic and advanced convnet models, Recurrent models, Attention mechanism, Transformers, Deep Representation learning, Deep Generative Models, Deep Reinforcement Learning (if time permits)
You should be familiar with Python, but you can learn during the course also
Course content is quite interesting, gives the practical and sufficient theoretical knowledge of almost all aspects of machine learning and that too from scratch in order to help the beginners. Pace of the course is moderate. The evaluation is one of the chillest. Exams are simple and MCQ based. AAs are given like anything. Almost 50-60% batch got AA I think. It is anice course for people wanting to start with machine learning or looking for a chill instiiute elective.The assignments and course projects are in groups so nothing to fear about and also the instructor is very chill. The load of the course is also very less during the semester.
• Home work – 20%
- 2 paper reviews – recent papers from ICCV/CVPR/NeurIPS/ICML/ICLR/ECCV/BMVC/TPAMI/IJCV/TNNLS (>=2018) – 10 x 2 = 20 % (team of 2 people)
• 1 Kaggle competition of image classification – 10%
• 1 course project – implement a paper, propose a new problem and implement, write a blog about a very recent paper – 25% (team of at least 3 people)
• Quiz (3) – 15 x 3 = 45%
Moderate if you are new to ML and quite easy if you already know it
prof's slides and lectures are sufficient. If you are stuck somewhere, you can find plenty of resources online on that topic

BB602-Reserach Philosophy


1

Review 1

By Bhuvan Aggarwal

Prof. Swati Patankar
The course is basically on research philosophy which helps in cultivating the research mindset and helping us get to know the proper path of research. The content seemes pretty easy, I had a strong liking for research and was looking for a chill institute elective. Didn't have any course reviews or seniors who had taken this previously but still because of the content ended up taking it.
The course cotent covers various aspects of research like how to read a research paper, how to conduct experiments, how to write papers, ethics of research, developing scientific mindset and so on. It was quite easy.
None
Luckily, it was one of the chillest, most fun and easiest course of my entire 3 years.The course content was made very interesting by the professor who was very fun to be taught by and the classes were very interactive. The evaluation was extremely chill based on just some assignments. The assignments were not some boring ones, they were actually quite interesting and stimulating, more of creative writing you can say like watching movie then answering questions and so on but were quite easy. Since these assignments were very open ended and there were no ideal answers, you end up getting full credit. Though the content is on overall research but there are some examples from biology here and there(even in some assignments) somewhat analogous to CE324. So, if you completely detest Biology by any chance then you might want to rethink about taking the course.

About

D-AMP stands for the Department Academic Mentorship Program. It comprises of two D-AMP Coordinators that oversee the program for the Civil Department and around thirty mentors. As the name suggests, we help students in their academic endeavours by providing them personal guidance and mentorship in order to smoothen their transition from freshmen year to sophomore year. As the D-AMP team, we always endeavour to go a step further and address the non academic and career related concerns of our mentees in order to provide a holistic mentorship experience.
